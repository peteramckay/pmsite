Meet the Roscoeheads

Our founding team has diverse experience in technology, media, and more. Below are individual bios for each of our team members -- Lawrence Patrick, Peter McKay, and Katrina Miles.

Lawrence C. Patrick III, Chief Executive Officer

Lawrence built his first website in 1994, before Netscape was launched. He went on to work on Mercury Center, the first daily news site in the U.S. Lawrence worked in Silicon Valley for Knight-Ridder New Media, launching several web-based products, including NewsLibrary.com, currently the world’s largest newspaper archive. He also worked closely on news products with Zip2, a startup founded by Elon and Kimbal Musk.

After leaving K-R, Lawrence co-founded Megaschool, one of the first digital textbook companies. He also served as founding president and CEO of Inspire USA Foundation, where he led the U.S. expansion of one of Australia’s leading anti-suicide charities. Before coming to Inspire, he was director of business development and strategy for Jazz at Lincoln Center, where he was responsible for new media, content distribution, intellectual property and corporate partnerships for the jazz world’s largest organization.

As president and CEO of the Black Alliance for Educational Options, Lawrence led the organization through dramatic early-stage growth. Under his leadership, BAEO helped shape public policy, launched several grassroots campaigns, and received a grant from the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation to create a national network of charter schools. Lawrence was selected for a fellowship to attend the Stanford University Graduate School of Business Executive Program for Nonprofit Leaders in 2006.

Peter McKay, Chief Product Officer

Peter is a former Wall Street Journal markets reporter. Over 11 years at the paper, he wrote extensively for both the web and print editions, including daily coverage of the stock-market crash in late 2008. In the months following that historic event, he provided market analysis for more than 100 broadcast outlets, including CNN, the BBC, Fox News, CNBC, San Francisco's KRON and New York's WCBS on the Journal's behalf. Peter was part of the Journal team that won a 2011 Gerald Loeb Award for breaking coverage of the stock market's "flash crash," and he was a finalist in the same category in 2007. He was part of the Journal staff that as a group won the 2002 Pulitzer Prize for breaking coverage of the 9/11 terror attacks. In 2002, he was also an ethics fellow at the Poynter Institute for Media Studies. In 2009, Florida A&M University's journalism school gave him the Thelma Thurston Gorham Distinguished Alumnus Award. Peter has significant operational responsibility at Roscoe Labs and oversees innovation and development for us.

Katrina Miles, Chief Strategy Officer

Katrina Miles is an award-winning television and feature documentary film producer. As Chief Strategic Officer she is responsible for creating, communicating, executing and sustaining strategic initiatives. She is also a consultant and board advisor for international NGO, The Smallest Seed, empowering widows and orphans in Africa, Central Asia and China. She also served as a communications consultant for executive offices of the National Trust for Historic Preservation. In addition, Katrina has helped to develop shows for Discovery Networks, The History Channel, Oregon Public Television, PBS Sprout, and WNET, New York’s main public TV affiliate. Katrina is active on several boards around New York City, and has written for the Detroit Free Press, the Philadelphia Inquirer and the Boston Globe. She is also the former executive director of the Lewis H. Latimer Fund and a Deputy Communications Director under former Florida Gov. Jeb Bush.
